<p><p>Magnetoresistive RAM (MRAM) is a non-volatile memory technology that uses magnetic states to store data, offering high speed, durability, and low power consumption. The market comprises two main types: Toggle MRAM, which employs magnetic tunneling junctions for data switching, providing moderate speed and endurance; and second-generation MRAM, known as Spin-Transfer Torque MRAM (STT-MRAM), which utilizes spin-polarized currents for faster and more energy-efficient operations. Both types cater to diverse applications, especially in embedded systems and data centers.</p></p>
