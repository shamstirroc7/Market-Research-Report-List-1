<p><p>Surfactants in the agrochemical market enhance the effectiveness of pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers by improving their spreading, wetting, and adherence properties. Nonionic surfactants are neutral and compatible with various formulations, promoting stability. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge, improving dispersion and emulsification. Cationic surfactants, positively charged, enhance adhesion and reduce evaporation. Amphoteric surfactants can function as either positive or negative, allowing for versatility in formulations. Together, these surfactant types optimize agrochemical performance and efficacy.</p></p>
