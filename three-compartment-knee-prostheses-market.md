<p><p>The three-compartment knee prostheses market is categorized into cement fixation and cement alternatives. Cement fixation involves using polymethyl methacrylate to bond the prosthesis to the bone, providing strong stability and immediate load-bearing capabilities. In contrast, cement alternatives, such as biologically compatible materials, promote bone ingrowth and are favored for their potential long-term performance and reduced risk of loosening. Both options cater to different patient needs, surgical preferences, and long-term outcomes in knee replacement surgeries.</p></p>
