<p><p>The Floating LNG terminal market comprises two main types: Floating LNG Import Terminals and Floating LNG Export Terminals. Floating LNG Import Terminals facilitate the reception, storage, and regasification of liquefied natural gas at coastal locations, enabling countries to access gas supplies without extensive infrastructure. In contrast, Floating LNG Export Terminals are designed for the liquefaction and export of natural gas, often enabling resource-rich countries to efficiently monetize their gas reserves. Both types enhance energy security and flexibility in global gas markets.</p></p>

<p><p>The floating LNG terminal market encompasses Floating Production Storage and Offloading units (FPSOs) and Floating Storage Regasification Units (FSRUs), playing crucial roles in the LNG supply chain. FPSOs are designed for offshore gas extraction and processing, storing liquid natural gas before transport. In contrast, FSRUs convert stored LNG back to gas, supplying energy to onshore networks.
