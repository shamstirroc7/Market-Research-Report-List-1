<p><p>The industrial turbines market comprises two primary types: gas turbines and steam turbines. Gas turbines are designed for high-efficiency power generation, leveraging natural gas or liquids, making them ideal for peaking and base-load power plants. In contrast, steam turbines utilize steam produced from heating water, typically in thermal power plants, to generate electricity. Both types play crucial roles in energy production, with gas turbines favored for rapid response and efficiency, while steam turbines are renowned for their reliability and ability to harness waste heat in combined cycle systems.</p></p>
